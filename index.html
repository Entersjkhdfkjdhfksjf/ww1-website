World War I

The Great War that reshaped human history

1914 - 1918

Human Cost

Military Deaths: ~9.7 million

Civilian Deaths: ~10 million

Total Wounded: ~21 million

The First World War represented an unprecedented scale of human loss, forever changing the way societies viewed warfare and international relations.

Global Changes

  • Collapse of four major empires
  • Rise of new nation-states
  • Women's suffrage movement acceleration
  • Industrial and technological revolution
  • New world order emergence

The war catalyzed dramatic social, political, and technological changes that would define the 20th century.

Lasting Legacy

The aftermath of World War I laid the groundwork for:

  • The League of Nations and modern international diplomacy
  • Major advances in medical treatment and technology
  • Fundamental changes in warfare and military strategy
  • The reshaping of global power dynamics
  • The seeds of World War II
